# UniAudit — LinkedIn DM Scripts

Connection request messages and cold DMs for each stakeholder type.
Keep connection requests under 300 characters. DMs under 150 words.

Variables: `{{uni_name}}`, `{{your_name}}`, `{{overall_score}}`, `{{top_issue}}`, `{{report_url}}`

---

## Admission Director

*Frame: You've done their homework for them. Lead with the specific finding.*

### Variation A — Direct / Data-Led

```
Hi [Name],

I recently audited {{uni_name}}'s admission pages as part of a study on how
universities present themselves to prospective students online.

The site scored {{overall_score}}/100. The biggest gap I found: {{top_issue}}.

I put together a short report with the full findings — thought it might be
useful given your role. Happy to share it if you're curious.

{{your_name}}
```

### Variation B — Curiosity Hook

```
Hi [Name],

Quick question: do you know how {{uni_name}}'s admission pages compare to
peer institutions on information completeness and navigation clarity?

We audited 40+ university admission sites recently and {{uni_name}} came up
in the data. A few things stood out that I thought you'd want to see.

Would it be useful if I sent over the report?

{{your_name}}
```

### Variation C — Peer Comparison Angle

```

serve prospective students at the decision stage.

Free to share the full findings if you'd find it useful.
```

## Messaging Tips

**Connection timing:** Connect first, wait 2–3 days, then send the DM.
Sending a DM with the connection request reduces acceptance rate.

**Profile optimization before outreach:** Ensure your LinkedIn profile
headline and summary clearly establish your credential (e.g., "University
Admission Website Auditor | Helping universities convert more applicants
online"). Prospects check your profile before responding.

**Best days to send:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9am–11am recipient's local time.

**Don't link in the first message:** LinkedIn's algorithm suppresses
messages with links. Mention the report and offer to share it — send the
link in the follow-up or reply thread.

**Voice notes:** For warm connections or second-degree contacts, a
30-second voice note dramatically outperforms text. Reference the specific
finding you saw and offer to send the report.
